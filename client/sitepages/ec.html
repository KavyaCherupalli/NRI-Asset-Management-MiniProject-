ENCUMBRANCE CERTIFICATE

EC: A legal document stating that a property is free of financial or legal liabilities.

Usability Of The Document

  • The encumbrance certificate is crucial paperwork to demonstrate that the subject property pertains to the specific individual and that there is no property dispute.
  • It attests to the property being free and clear of any obligations.
  • The sub-office registrar's where the specific land has been certified is where one can acquire the EC for a ground.

When Do You Need An Encumbrance Certificate?

  • When buying a home, a non-encumbrance certificate is required to prove that you are dealing with the right person and that there are no outstanding loans on the property.
  • When you apply for a home loan to purchase a home, banks will often want an encumbrance certificate before accepting your application.
  • If you use your provident fund to make a down payment on a house, your employer will seek the EC.
  • Following the property purchase, the owner must have the ownership transfer recorded in the official records via property mutation.
  • When selling a home, the seller must apply for and obtain the paperwork from the government's records to display it to the buyer.
